The Core Innovation

Optimised Refrigerant Supply

Standard thermostats only monitor room temperature — causing compressors to run far longer than necessary. enPact monitors the superheat at the evaporator to optimise every compressor cycle.

Compressors account for 80–90% of a refrigeration system's energy consumption. By monitoring superheat at the evaporator via sensors ① and ②, enPact optimises every compressor cycle — delivering savings of up to 40%.
Phase 1 — Monitor

Assess Superheat

Sensors measure coolant in/out temperatures to calculate superheat. System detects when evaporator reaches maximum cooling capacity.

Phase 2 — Interrupt

Close Solenoid Valve

enPact closes the solenoid valve, stopping refrigerant flow. The compressor halts — preventing energy waste when cooling is already at maximum.

Phase 3 — Harvest

Latent Cooling Recovery

Remaining refrigerant in the evaporator continues absorbing heat — extracting every unit of cooling energy that would otherwise be wasted.

Phase 4 — Restart

Soft-Start Compressor

When superheat drops below the lower threshold, the solenoid reopens and the compressor restarts with soft-start protection.

The system uses an energy trading platform model with three temperature sensors that work together to optimise the condensing unit/pack system — the component responsible for 80–90% of a refrigeration system's energy consumption.

Step 01

Assess Superheat

enPact measures the refrigeration temperature at sensors 1 and 2 (coolant in/out) to determine when the evaporator has reached maximum cooling capacity. This calculates the superheat — the key indicator of system efficiency.

Step 02

Interrupt Compressor Cycle

Once maximum capacity is detected, enPact interrupts the electrical supply to the solenoid valve, closing it. This in turn stops the compressor, preventing energy waste during periods when the system is already at optimal cooling.

Step 03

Maximise Latent Cooling

enPact keeps the solenoid valve closed while the remaining refrigerant in the evaporator continues to absorb heat. This maximises the latent cooling energy already in the system — energy that would otherwise be wasted.

Step 04

Restart When Needed

When the refrigerant is fully used and the superheat drops to its lower threshold, enPact restores electrical supply to the solenoid valve, reopening it and restarting the compressor. The set temperature is always maintained as the primary objective.
